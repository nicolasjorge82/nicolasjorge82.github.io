      DSGE and the real estate market

      June 17, 2021 in papers

      Back in 2009, for my master’s thesis I decided to apply a DSGE model to analyze the real state market prices in Argentina.

      Back in 2009, while thinking about possible projects for my master degree thesis, I came across some very interesting applications for Dynamic Stochastic General Equilibrium (DSGE) models that analyzed the relationship on macroeconomic variables and real estate market prices. Reasonably, the topic was being discussed in many papers given the emerging of the sub-prime mortgage crisis in the US.

      Inspired by those works, for my thesis I decided to apply a DSGE model to analyze the situation in Argentina, which was interesting because the apartment prices were booming, and there were some questions on whether this was a consequence of strong fundamentals or there was a pricing bubble that could burst at any moment.

      The model I had chosen was based on Iacovello (2004), although with some changes to make it useful for countries with high inflation (Escude, 2007). The modelling was rather straightforward thanks to the Dynare package, which allowed to code the model in MATLAB, and estimate it through the maximum likelihood estimator.
