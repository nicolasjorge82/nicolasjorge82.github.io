      My degree thesis, tons of fun, a while ago.

      Is #TBT still a thing? Anyway, I wanted to share two old articles in this post and the next one. In this case, my degree thesis titled in spanish “Dinámica no lineal y caos en el mercado cambiario: Un análisis empírico para Argentina”, which I wrote in 2004. I remember really enjoying working on it. The conclusions are not very relevant today, but it allowed me to learn on interesting topics.

      The main objective was to analyze the exchange rate in Argentina by applying econometric tests for non-linear dynamics and chaos. The first was the BSD test (Brock, Dechert and Scheinkman), which null hypothesis is that of independence and identical distribution (IID). After filtering out the linear dynamic by means of the Box-Jenkins method, all structure that’s left must be non-linear. The best linear model found was an ARIMA(2,1,0), and the testing of the residuals strongly rejected the IID hypothesis, meaning that there was some nonlinear structure in the series.

      The second test, NEGM, (Ellner, Nychka, Gallant y McCaffrey, 1992) implies running a non-parametric estimation of the Lyapunov exponent, which is a measure of how sensitive a process is to the initial conditions. The strategy proposed by the authors is the estimation of a feedforward linear single-hidden layer neural network. Given that it is not obvious which are the right parameters for the neural network, many combinations are tried, and the best model is chosen by means of the Bayesian Information Criterion.

      This second test was somewhat challenging. I remember adapting some parts of the FORTRAN 77 code to match my compiler, and having to wait for many hours to get the results on my old Pentium 4. In this case, the result was the absence of a chaotic dynamic in the series.
